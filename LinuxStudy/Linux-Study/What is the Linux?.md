**The Core Architecture: Kernel vs OS**

Technically speaking, "Linux" is not a complete operating system. It is just the kernel.
Think of the kernel as the engine of a car. An engine alone won't get you to work; you need a chassis, wheels, seats, and a dashboard. In the software world, when you combine the Linux kernel with utility tools, graphical interfaces, and package managers, you get a complete Linux Distribution (or "Distro") such as Ubuntu, Debian, or Fedora.
The system operates in three distinct layers:
The kernel: Directs your hardware (CPU, memory, disks) and translates software commands into physical actions.
